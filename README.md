A copy of our current tiering criteria can be found below:
Our way of tiering units is meant to be as close to objective as possible. While maintaining true objectivity is impossible, dicussed below here is our criteria for how we judge units for their placement.
Of course, you are free to disagree or view things differently than us. However, the point of this little article is to explain our viewpoint and why we place units when they do in a more concrete manner.
Just a few things to note below...

ALL UNITS ARE ASSUMED 25 LUCK W/ MAX REIN BUFFS AND STANDARD BUFFS

NUMBERS WISE: Magic number for LWs when translated between our calc and in-game stuff tends to be at around 2.2mil to "pass." Though having such a low cap is obviously far from ideal, which can end up docking points.
Killer ranges: Generally to "pass" a character would like to hit at least 1/3-1/2 of the same, so around 180ish characters. 180 flat is not good, it still docks points, but it would be "passing" in the sense of a D on a report card.

A) Unit cost is ignored, as is the capability for one unit to replace another.
Take for instance Mima and A6& Rumia. Objectively, A6& Rumia is simply Mima but worse in almost every way (except acc) despite being the same cost, but this is not held particularly against Rumia. 
Our viewpoint is that this list simply shows each units' capabilities without assuming the existence of units that completely outclass them, whether or not in their own element.
As another point, F1 Reimu is infamously outclassed by the modern yin/sun options such as Gengetsu, L80 Kasen and Cf1# Reimu, but she remains in High A despite their existence as what she provides is enough to get her there.
Trying to make a tierlist that factors in both cost and replacement, which is essentially a SYP (should you pull) list muddies waters and complicates things a lot. 
Our mindset is it would be better to have a (close to) objective resource that outlines each unit for what they provide, and then having players discuss and make decisions based then. 
(Just in case this didn't get the point across, imagine being new and having A6& Rumia, but not Mima. In the system outlined above, Rumia would be placed in C and you would be made to think she is unusable garbage when she still has A-worthy capabilities.)
Where being replaced/outclassed does become a problem is when story cards can replace units, as at that point the unit becomes more of a waste of a slot and resources.

B) The primary source of judgement on this list is capability in Divergent Sprits.
Characters that can Duo or Solo farm DS stages in 3 turns, or provide a lot in DS120 stages, are the most favourably looked upon by us as that is the content we have to go on that is the most consistent and commonly made to evaluate units with.
If a character needs is very trio-reliant, that is held against them as it means they do not have the damage, support, breaks, and/or utility to go solo/duo and thus need more to get them working.
We are aware that most people do not try to make 10sp duo farms and most people use trios. The point of the tierlist isn't to be for those who do, because those people generally don't need a tierlist.
Instead, it is to have a semi-consistent baseline to hold units against and judge them accordingly.
Now, as for Eternal Battle, it is also ranked to an extent, but with far less 'points' scored than DS. This is because it is wildly different, being much more prone to having specific units be shilled, and is very inconsistent and uncommon.
It also needs very different requirements, thus trying to account it too much proves unuseful. 
As for gamemode like Arena, Tower, and Moriya Unmapped: these gamemode are simply too easy and have different requirements, thus they are not accounted for (also giving usually worse rewards.)

Extra Note on how Pros/Cons are Listed, Going down the Unit List
Generally, we tend to judge the units in EX-S on a harsher scale compared to units below them. This is due to a combination of factors, being that lower tier units generally have less to talk about and overall are less capable of accomplishing any designated task,
while at the same time units in higher tiers are supposed to be more universal. Anything that hurts said universability will be judged in a harsher lens as it tends to go further in limiting their potential than units below them.
For example, C3 Tenshi not having Attack Anomalies is docked as a con against her while it isn't mentioned for anyone below B. This is because, as an S tier unit (as of writing) she has the POTENTIAL to be great, but has a major thorn
in her side in that she is extremely weak to buff cleansing situations. Attack anomalies would have greatly helped her in retaining her buffs (like C3 Yuyuko) but as she can't she ends up being considerably more reliant on party members to function as a result.
Meanwhile, for your average B tier unit, they generally don't even have half of Tenshi's damage potential, so missing Attack Anomalies isn't a big deal in the end. This can apply to anything, like being susceptible to paralyze or having inoptimal skill layouts.

Explaining how tiering is done:
Tiering tldr: Tiering is done by using sort of a checklist to combine objective factors and to see how well they are in addition to what is missing

Step 1: What does the unit do?
In this step look at the unit's kit and observe what they actually do. High damage? High breaks? Support? Debuffing? Etc High damage, somewhere in the 4mil+ on realistic with 1000 base defense. 6+ breaks on decent cooldowns. Etc. 
Examples:
Unit has poor last word damage, good debuffing, solid buffing and breaking. That is what they are designed to do.
Unit has great aoe damage, aoe breaking on all spells, damage, support, and solid debuffing. 

Step 2: Take said strengths and compare them to objective in practice elements.
Take those strengths, and then see their "realistics" and how good said strengths actually are. This step is the longest. 
Assumptions: As is safe to assume and easy to tier for. We assume a player has the needed number of bis cards and the highly recommended cards that 
were stated would be amazing and should be farmed/pulled for. Its hard to tier for anything but this, as say we ignored it but you were a lucky few who kept 
grinding and found themselves with a lucky card or two that can really carry a unit. Suddenly for you a unit would be much better. So in that instance who
do we tier for? The common player who will be attempting to catch up with you or you who the common player will eventually reach (hopefully). 
As ds is valued by far the most, we will judge based on given factors like buff remove every gauge and such.

Is what they give on paper worth it compared to what you could be doing / will be fighting. Are their pros valuable for the current tier given?
