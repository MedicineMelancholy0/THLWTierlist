Tierlist Definitions

Damage reqs: Damage reqs are simply the practical damage cap. Which is about 2.2million for all intensive purposes in modern ds. Notice how our meme calc sheet has very few aoes to even remotely hit that high. This is because you have many additional factors in a real gameplay setting. What is say, a 650k aoe at cap can quickly become 300k at 5/12. And then 600k again with proper rank ii. And then 2.4mil on full break. So on and so forth. It is important to keep in mind there are no blanket statements to the practical damage cap. So ultimately one will need to test for themselves as we do. Just keep in mind a unit's own buffs can very much matter for this practical damage. So almost nothing is created equal.

Overflow: Bullets not being ordered perfectly linear in a spell. ie. the line 6 blind break appearing earlier than expected etc. Refer to strat guide for more info

BIS: Best in slot. The story card that offers the most damage (usually a 90%+ buff)

[Bullet] Nerf/Cuck: Bullet type restrictions, usually in Divergent Spirits 120

DS120: Divergent Spirits Stages Level 120

EB: Eternal Battle. Static modes break down into: EBR = Eternal Battle Ranked, EBL = Eternal Battle Limited

NLM: Bullet line with 34%+ base crit rate

Prae Card: Hunting the Hunter Luck/Fate card. Mostly renowned for its potent Atk Locked Yin Atk Up + Yin Def Down card.

BS: Barrier Status Luck/Fate card. Mostly known for -3 Def, 50% light effect.

Shansoku: 50% body, 50% liquid, -2 yang def card

CQ: Challenge Quest. Divergent Spirits, Eternal Battle (Limited, Ranked is seperate), Advanced Challenge Stages, and Elemental EX Nightmare.

Mixed: Having both Yin/Yang divide your spellcard/Last Word

Soupy: Juggling 3-4 Elements across a single attack or set of attacks

Rainbow: 4+ Elements in an attack or set of attacks

Sfes: Another term for Ultra Festival

Scaling: Refers to Hard/Slice scaling in certain attacks

Unbind: Lock Cleanse/Debuff Cleanse

Boost Scale: Spellcard effects that increase based on P used rather than awakening level (relatively uncommon)

A-Fes: Character with A universe code (NOT including those ending in &)

Rein: Reincarnation/Fantasy rebirth. Funfact rein as a term was made in 2021

Official Tiering Criteria

Our way of tiering units is meant to be as close to objective as possible. While maintaining true objectivity is impossible, dicussed below here is our criteria for how we judge units for their placement. Of course, you are free to disagree or view things differently than us. However, the point of this little article is to explain our viewpoint and why we place units when they do in a more concrete manner. Just a few things to note below...

ALL UNITS ARE ASSUMED 25 LUCK W/ MAX REIN BUFFS AND STANDARD BUFFS

NUMBERS WISE: Magic number for LWs when translated between our calc and in-game stuff tends to be at around 2.2mil to "pass." Though having such a low cap is obviously far from ideal, which can end up docking points.

Killer ranges: Generally to "pass" a character would like to hit at least 1/3-1/2 of the same, so around 180ish characters. 180 flat is not good, it still docks points, but it would be "passing" in the sense of a D on a report card.

1. Unit cost is ignored, as is the capability for one unit to replace another.

Take for instance Mima and A6& Rumia. Objectively, A6& Rumia is simply Mima but worse in almost every way (except acc) despite being the same cost, but this is not held particularly against Rumia. Our viewpoint is that this list simply shows each units' capabilities without assuming the existence of units that completely outclass them, whether or not in their own element. As another point, F1 Reimu is infamously outclassed by the modern yin/sun options such as Gengetsu, L80 Kasen and Cf1# Reimu, but she remains in High A despite their existence as what she provides is enough to get her there. Trying to make a tierlist that factors in both cost and replacement, which is essentially a SYP (should you pull) list muddies waters and complicates things a lot. Our mindset is it would be better to have a (close to) objective resource that outlines each unit for what they provide, and then having players discuss and make decisions based then. (Just in case this didn't get the point across, imagine being new and having A6& Rumia, but not Mima. In the system outlined above, Rumia would be placed in C and you would be made to think she is unusable garbage when she still has A-worthy capabilities.) Where being replaced/outclassed does become a problem is when story cards can replace units, as at that point the unit becomes more of a waste of a slot and resources.


2. The primary source of judgement on this list is capability in Divergent Sprits.

Characters that can Duo or Solo farm DS stages in 3 turns, or provide a lot in DS120 stages, are the most favourably looked upon by us as that is the content we have to go on that is the most consistent and commonly made to evaluate units with. If a character needs is very trio-reliant, that is held against them as it means they do not have the damage, support, breaks, and/or utility to go solo/duo and thus need more to get them working. We are aware that most people do not try to make 10sp duo farms and most people use trios. The point of the tierlist isn't to be for those who do, because those people generally don't need a tierlist. Instead, it is to have a semi-consistent baseline to hold units against and judge them accordingly. Now, as for Eternal Battle, it is also ranked to an extent, but with far less 'points' scored than DS. This is because it is wildly different, being much more prone to having specific units be shilled, and is very inconsistent and uncommon. It also needs very different requirements, thus trying to account it too much proves unuseful. As for gamemode like Arena, Tower, and Moriya Unmapped: these gamemode are simply too easy and have different requirements, thus they are not accounted for (also giving usually worse rewards.)


Extra Note on how Pros/Cons are Listed, Going down the Unit List

Generally, we tend to judge the units in EX-S on a harsher scale compared to units below them. This is due to a combination of factors, being that lower tier units generally have less to talk about and overall are less capable of accomplishing any designated task, while at the same time units in higher tiers are supposed to be more universal. Anything that hurts said universability will be judged in a harsher lens as it tends to go further in limiting their potential than units below them. For example, C3 Tenshi not having Attack Anomalies is docked as a con against her while it isn't mentioned for anyone below B. This is because, as an S tier unit (as of writing) she has the POTENTIAL to be great, but has a major thorn in her side in that she is extremely weak to buff cleansing situations. Attack anomalies would have greatly helped her in retaining her buffs (like C3 Yuyuko) but as she can't she ends up being considerably more reliant on party members to function as a result. Meanwhile, for your average B tier unit, they generally don't even have half of Tenshi's damage potential, so missing Attack Anomalies isn't a big deal in the end. This can apply to anything, like being susceptible to paralyze or having inoptimal skill layouts.


With that out of the way, how we judge units is as follows:

Explaining how tiering is done:

Tiering tldr: Tiering is done by using sort of a checklist to combine objective factors and to see how well they are in addition to what is missing

Step 1: What does the unit do?

In this step look at the unit's kit and observe what they actually do. High damage? High breaks? Support? Debuffing? Etc High damage, somewhere in the 4mil+ on realistic with 1000 base defense. 6+ breaks on decent cooldowns. Etc.

Examples:

Unit has poor last word damage, good debuffing, solid buffing and breaking. That is what they are designed to do.

Unit has great aoe damage, aoe breaking on all spells, damage, support, and solid debuffing.

Step 2: Take said strengths and compare them to objective in practice elements.

Take those strengths, and then see their "realistics" and how good said strengths actually are. This step is the longest.

Assumptions: As is safe to assume and easy to tier for. We assume a player has the needed number of bis cards and the highly recommended cards that were stated would be amazing and should be farmed/pulled for. Its hard to tier for anything but this, as say we ignored it but you were a lucky few who kept grinding and found themselves with a lucky card or two that can really carry a unit. Suddenly for you a unit would be much better. So in that instance who do we tier for? The common player who will be attempting to catch up with you or you who the common player will eventually reach (hopefully). As ds is valued by far the most, we will judge based on given factors like buff remove every gauge and such.

Examples:

Lastword damage is especially bad 1.6mil cap. Realistics (non fb def down included buffs included) unit hits a resounding 10% of the usual 1.3mil for modern level 100. Further more unit shows unreliability in killer. Due to the lack of damage, their ability to duo is extremely low.

Debuffing is the highest in the game, -10 given proper card, skill use, and last word use. Additionally, it is extremely reliable.

Killer is also very low, 30% of ds and 33% of the game in the current state. Unreliable.

Buffs are solid for one gauge. Post buff removal and due to the lack of buffs from ability and pre effect their support falls of a cliff. Card usage is quite reliable due to quick + breaking utility being good. Making them worth running in that role.

in trios, which is the only place this unit can function, such things are not of concern, so unit will fall into around B tier at this stage.

Unit has 100% storedpower for 3t, perfectly reliable assuming their Rank I buff throughout gauges is at least competent.

Unit needs x amount of buffs to make it to the expected damage requirements.

Step 3: Not at first glance/advanced checks (at this point, this takes me the most time)

This step is usually almost exclusively a check of utility and is a thing ever since mvmu was invented. Youre basically checking "does everything agree" or finer details. If unit is shaping up to be a B tier aoe spell nuker with a st lw. Can they actually do that in practice before in practice tests happen. Is what they give on paper worth it compared to what you could be doing / will be fighting. Are their pros valuable for the current tier given?

Example: Unit has 100% stored power, tied to barrier which is unfortunate. However, the remainder of their kit's buffs dont let this be a true positive When used as a buffer with their damage skill they lack the other tier one buffs for the role so youd need to bring a tier 1 buffer shafting them leading to them being replaced in practice by a truly relevant t1 buffer.

Example: Unit has an amazing aoe spell which can somewhat supplement the loss of an aoe last word. However, their raw speed makes it near impossible to get off in pratice. They just can't abuse the thing that would save them. In niche situations it can work out, however it is something to hold against them. So their pro of strong aoe spell doesnt pass the "is it valuable" question leading to another point of inconsistency. Resulting in a lower tier.

Example: Unit is pretty good in standard 3t situations. But beyond that scope. Unit also has incredible solo abilities thanks to their amazing yet thus far undervalued utility. Stuns, barriers, defense down to counter the spam of enemy defense up. Spells that reach their requirements. It is all there, as they have these abilities in addition to their already determined to be valuable abilities they should be given extra props for them.

Example: Imma be real, this unit is entirely damage, they have little to nothing that isn't obvious and the majority of the kit agrees with itself for their role. Damage is high enough for resisted nuking though. Giving them surprising out of element preformance.

Step 4: Tier specific questions.

You see those silly tier descriptions, do another check to see if the unit fits said definition to avoid small issues that can be hard to weed out.

Example: They have perfect preformance inside of their element. Geniuely amazing. However, based on their kit with the buffs and such in addition to 60% damage to resist they actually have the ability to nuke outside of element. Combined their killers theyre actually really really good at this.

Step 5: In practice

By this point, the unit is on the tierlist, however we still may not know if our assumptions and calculators included everything. So they are tested thoroughly and if nothing disagrees with our assumptions they remain where they are. If something changes, such the unit's kit does actually agree with itself and we just didn't think about it well enough. Then the tiering will change. As you can imagine this can be caused by anything. F1 mors can actually work in practice and was assumed not to because her t1 buffs suck? Well turns out they dont suck as badly, or rather, her scaling with the realistc t1 buffs she can obtain in most comps does actually get her by when calcs say they wouldnt.

Example: This unit can actually be used as the sole damage dealer when it looked like they couldn't. Their lack of t1 buffs is less of an issue than as calced and their ability to pull their damage off is better than calcs would assume.

Step 6: Congratulate yourself on tiering with as little bias as you can possibly think of.

Please ask any questions, this is heavily abridged. More accurately, the first two steps are a massive list of yes or no questions for the most part. This is just a simple way to think how we do.

FAQ

What sets us apart from other Tierlists?

We operate on a small but dedicated team of players who are open to any and all feedback, actively play and calculate new things while remaining adherent to a strict set of guidelines while remaining transparent about everything we do. If you need something, just ask. We will explain everything.

What is Utility?

Utility can be defined as support that doesn't directly increase damage. For example, stuns and party accuracy between gauges are some of the biggest sources of utility. Utility also qualifies as anomaly breaks (which are generally to be expected), party barrier restoration, party unbinds, useful shots (look at the getsu sisters) and things like party poison (usually more useful than not nowadays) qualify as utility. Gengetsu currently has the best universal utility in the game as a measuring stick.

WHY are units assumed to be 25 luck and without the +2p buffs from 150 luck?

We cannot tier around individual accounts. If we want to assume everyone starts at 2p, solving half of their issues, everyone would be in SS or EX. At the cost of a $10,000 hole in your wallet. This helps nobody. Therefore we will not be accounting for it. Anybody who says this is dumb: seriously ask yourself, what use is a tierlist catered exclusively to whales?

Will PhFes Switch Links be taken into consideration for tiering?

No. Even though the switch links (40% stat increase, skill 4) are undeniably helpful... they don't help with our criteria of 10sp duos. The fundamental issue is that the switch links tether the character to being switched, meaning they have to rely on the vanguard exfes to do something for at least one gauge if they want to duo ("trios" should be trivial but that is a low bar) however most ExFes are... pretty bad at dealing with a whole gauge by themselves without outside support with the exceptions of EX Sakuya, Yuyuko and Seiran. So it's still functionally a trio at the end of the day and they will be ranked as if they are normal units with 3 skills and no stat switch links.

Why are ST LWs Bad?

99% Of this games' Challenge Content is more than 1 enemy. Solo target last words... target 1 enemy. Ignoring how most ST LWs in this game just suck, it is just inefficient by design and a waste of a unit slot. They barely ever do what a potent ST Spell, Last Word or even an AoE Spellcard can do. The few times they are useful, they tend not to be the most efficient solution, or it is very very niche. So it is simply docked as a con.

Isn't this a Global Tierlist? Why are JP Characters and Reins added so soon?

Becise Medi is very imaptient (don't ask this won't be fixed)

Why is Parallel Presence Killer not Valued Much if it has So Many People?

Long story short: it isn't actually that wide, doesn't have that many stages, and is often wiped by killer change leading niche killers like soul or fairy to be overall more useful somehow.

Why is being reliant on Damage to Eff/Being Mono Ele Bad?

Most units are already perfectly capable of nuking in their element, sometimes even without killers. Most content nowadays has around 2-3 different elements of weakness for each gauge. Especially with killer change content, units might have a hard time nuking other enemies, especially if resisted. If they can already over kill their primary element but can't do anything about the others (damage to res), they're effectively tethered to it and need more support/nukers. Thus it is counted as at least a / (mixed/double edged sword.) Extra Note: Devs tend to directly nerf base power with higher dmg to eff, which is why neutral dmg tends to fall off a cliff for these units.

Thlw is a game without the most amount of obtainable options at the end of the day. Saving unit slots is extremely valuable as a result. So units with incredible aoe spells are highly valued. Additionally they also let you run purely supportive units, making the game much easier. And making the game easier is an example of a better unit.

But what about Arena or Tower? Or MU?

The issue with both of these is that neither are challenging enough to provide much use here. Additionally with Arena, the rewards are too shit to consider. They are also just fundamentally different as clear content. Arena is just a Dmg to Eff check, while Tower is an 'Anything Goes' type deal with its damage sponges. Neither metric would help much here.

What about classes? Isn't it expected that supp/heal/dbf units won't do much dmg?

Classes in this game are basically cosmetic with the exception of class locked cards and nerfs. Everyone does damage. Ever since the olden days of otohime and ekari, it has been proven that even "non-offensive" classes are more than capable of nuking. Therefore having a bad class is not an excuse for having low damage. It just sucks all the same.

How is buff cleansing accounted for?

Characters who are particular weak to buff cleansing. (ie. all their buffs are just from skills with long cds) ARE put lower on the list. Buff cleansing is now a normal expected part of every stage and lacking shorter cooldowns, anom buffs, good spell buffs, abilities, etc. Will put units behind noticeably. As such units are typically placed lower depending on how bad they are countered by buff cleanse. Do they have good enough inlines? How many buffs do they need for damage? Can they run cards to help? These questions are things we ask when a unit has buff removal issues and might soften the blow so keep that in mind.

Why are the tiers so bloated?

As of writing this game has over 400 characters. We get 1, sometimes 2 every week. Having tiers be bloated is unavoidable no matter how we try to raise, drop, or judge units. Judging them too harshly based on arbitrary difficulty that doesn't actually exists helps nobody. Blame NN for shitting out units like they just ate taco bell.

Ok, why not make in-between tiers then, like an A+ or B-?

The reason why we don't make these is because they don't solve much except tier bloat. Most differences between units aren't that minute to warrant such ultra-specificity. Thus we have decided not to add these.

Why is x unit so high when y unit completely replaces her?

This tierlist does not judge units based on how replaceable they are by other units because it's not really that helpful if you don't have Y unit. It only starts being a problem in our eyes when x unit is completely replaced by cards.

How is ranked EBR accounted for? Isn't it silly to see mid-low tier units being represented a lot by ranked EB?

As of writing there are 2 ranked EBs and both are pretty different requirement wise. They are also pretty fundamentally different for the rest of the game as well. For what it's worth, they are accounted for, but with low priority so it doesn't impact much on the TL. This may change if they decide to increase the rate that they are produced. It also plays into how lower tier units aren't necessarily bad but rather function within specific niches, which is what Ranked EB essentially is. As for NON-RANKED Eternal Battle stages, that being EBL (Eternal Battle Limited), those are factored into tier considerations. As it qualifies as Challenge Quests for our purposes and is consistent enough to what we already have to be included.

Why is x unit so low when they can take advantage of weakness inflict?

When you bring unit + weakness inflict, you bring 2 units. At that point, what's stopping you from just bringing two other EX tiers? Being reliant on another unit to fulfill one of your gaps is a sign of weakness.

Will you ever make a story card tierlist?

No and we never will. The issue is, by design, theyre all supposed to be puzzle pieces. So the only real way to rate them is how often they can be used as such.

Best of the best. Their flaws and issues almost entirely ignorable/worth for what they provide. Additionally, these units are extremely recommended for players to obtain as soon as possible regardless of their current unit collection. Units of this caliber are universally proven useful and consistent inside their element. As well as outside their element to a reasonable extent that they (the unit in question) can replace units in the S/A tier even when they themselves lack content favoring.

SS Tier Explanation

The tier in between EX and S, representing great units that have issues preventing them from being universal options.

Units in this tier are typically units who either

A. Have universal aspects to them (not to the extent of EX) and lack the heights of EX tier in favored content and/or

B. Units who have equal heights to EX but lack the consistency to reach them/outside ele performance to justify being raised

Units are defined as universal when they are the easiest and most consistent units to run in content that does favor them as much as it doesn't.

×
S Tier Explanation

Generally good units that have some notable flaws but still perform well in opportunities expected of them such as effective elements. These units' flaws prevent them from being universal answers who always have a place in any stage.

Units in S typically are reliable in a multitude of facets but not universal in any capacity. Units are defined as universal when they are the easiest and most consistent units to run in content that does favors them as much as it doesn't. One may find issues with using these units as answers to all favorable content however, they are reliable in what they preform which can be favored or unfavored.

×
A Tier Explanation

A tier units have one fundamental idea being that they are less consistent than the s tier units with the same heights/abuseable strengths as them. As opposed to s tier whom are universal in these strengths without noteable flaws to prevent them and B tier whom lack the heights too many areas to form this consistent use case that A and S have. Most obvious example of this is AoE damage where units without abusable AoEs will drop very quickly unless they have something specific to save them (such as L80 Miko and B3 Shinmy's breaking and supportive abilities)

×
B Tier Explanation

Same as A tier, but tend to have more flaws.

Units in this tier have similar strengths to the units in A but more weaknesses/even less consistency.

Inconsistency is defined as: A situation where the units strengths can match well into one stage with specific gimmicks but not in others. While A tier has a lot of these units, the units in B have even more flaws and often less niches.

This mostly boils down to 1. inconsistent AoE damage (usually by virtue of lacking p0 killer) or lacking utility (mostly breaks). Other flaws can include underwhelming killer sets or lacking buffs throughout their kit which means most of the units here are effectively limited to one gauge and don't fit the 3t 10/15sp farm criteria OR they are only used for one thing such as niche shill content or breaks without support/damage that allow them to go up. For example L80 Patchouli technically has a powerful buff throughout her kit (stored power) but her innate support and utility is bad on top of horrible AoE spellcard damage and a LW nuke that has never really proven itself hence her placement. Meanwhile characters like B3 Raiko can be used throughout all 3 gauges but are very limited to being only a supporter/breaker without the heights of, say, B3 Shinmyoumaru (6/3 breaks instead of 7/7) to compensate hence she remains here.

×
C Tier Explanation

Friends who are only really solid in early game and fall off pretty quickly after your box starts getting more developed.

Units in this tier typically lack the ability to have above one niche. They are often specialists who can do one thing semi-reliably. In addition they have other aspects to them that while not noteable aren't useless.

×
D Tier Explanation

Friends who will likely not be the best option for any content aside from very new accounts.

Similar to the C tier, however units here lack even that one niche reliably/have a reliable niche and no semblance of a kit left to do anything else. That said, they still come close. VERY occasionally they may be able to do something in 1 specific pairing or situation but that is mostly due to pure chance and having the stars align rather than being a testament to their kit.

This is the point where my notes will go to shit because the unit in question doesn't have a lot to them that isnt obvious.

×
E Tier Explanation

Friends who are likely not the best scenario for any situation and are mostly only used for memes.

Effectively the D tier however they do not reach the bare minimum in some aspects requiring units to do additional work in those aspects. In other words, D tier units who actively make your team worse due to what they lack outside their occasional niche. Exceptions: L1 Sannyo, L1 Tsukasa, L1 Megumu

Yukkuri Shitte Ne

These characters technically don't qualify for the main list due to a lack of anything important but since it's funny we gave them their own list.
